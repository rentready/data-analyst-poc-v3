# PRO Terminology - Property Management Industry Terms

## Прошник (Proshnik)

**Definition**: "Прошник" is an informal slang term used in the Russian property management industry. It refers to "прошлый жилец" (previous tenant) - a tenant who has already moved out of the property.

**Context**: This term is commonly used by property managers, maintenance teams, and operations staff when discussing unit turnover and tenant history.

**Usage Examples**:
- "Нужно проверить, что прошник оставил после себя" - "Need to check what the previous tenant left behind"
- "Прошник не оплатил последний месяц" - "The previous tenant didn't pay for the last month"
- "Ущерб от прошника составил $500" - "Damage from the previous tenant was $500"

**Related Terms**:
- Turn (оборот) - The process of preparing a unit for a new tenant after the previous one moves out
- Vacancy (вакансия) - An empty unit without a tenant
- Move-out inspection (осмотр при выезде) - Final inspection when a tenant leaves

**Business Process**: When a "прошник" moves out, the property management team initiates a "turn" process, which includes inspection, cleaning, repairs, and preparation for the next tenant.
